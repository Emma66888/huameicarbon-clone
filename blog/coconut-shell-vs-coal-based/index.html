Coconut Shell vs Coal Based Activated Carbon: Key Differences

Choosing between coconut shell and coal-based activated carbon is one of the most common decisions buyers face. Both materials produce high-quality activated carbon, but they excel in different applications. This article breaks down the key differences to help you decide.

Raw Material & Manufacturing

Coconut Shell: Made from coconut shells (a renewable resource), carbonized at high temperatures and activated with steam. Hard, dense, and rich in micropores.

Coal Based: Made from bituminous or anthracite coal. Available in a wider range of forms (granular, pellet, powder) and macro/mesopore distributions.

Comparison Table

ParameterCoconut ShellCoal Based
HardnessVery High (>97%)Medium to High (90-95%)
Iodine Value800 - 1200 mg/g600 - 1050 mg/g
Pore StructureMicroporous dominantMicro + Mesopores
CostHigherLower / More cost-effective
Ash Content2 - 5%5 - 15%
Best ForGold recovery, premium water, gas adsorptionWastewater, bulk water treatment, air filters

When to Choose Coconut Shell

  • Gold recovery (CIP/CIL) — high hardness resists attrition in carbon-in-leach systems
  • Premium drinking water filters — low ash, high adsorption capacity
  • Gas phase adsorption — VOCs, H2S, solvent recovery
  • Applications requiring long service life and minimal fines

When to Choose Coal Based

  • Wastewater treatment — COD/BOD removal, cost-effective for large volumes
  • Pellet form for gas phase — VOC removal, air purification systems
  • Amine filtration and decolorization applications
  • Projects with tight budget constraints needing reliable performance
